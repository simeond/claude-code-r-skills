## Examiner Psychology Notes

1. **Examiners respect honest acknowledgment.** Saying "I haven't done X, which is a gap" is better than defending a weak position.
2. **Null results require careful framing.** Don't oversell; be precise about what you can and cannot rule out.
3. **Connect to broader economics.** Have theoretical frameworks in mind beyond your specific application.
4. **Be prepared for "so what?"** Have concrete implications for stakeholders with appropriate caveats.
5. **Show growth.** "What would you do differently?" is an opportunity to demonstrate maturity.

---

## Formatting guidelines

1. **Questions in quotes** — Use the exact phrasing an examiner would use.
2. **Answers in blockquotes** — Makes them easy to practice.
3. **"Why this works" bullets** — Strategic meta-commentary.
4. **Follow-ups indented** — Show the exchange chain.
5. **Tables for quick-fire** — Easy to scan and memorise.
6. **Numbers table at end** — Ready reference for key statistics.

## Length calibration

| Scope | Target words | Minimum questions |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Full paper | 8,000–12,000 | 20–25 |
| Single chapter | 4,000–6,000 | 12–15 |

## Coverage checklist

Before finalising, verify:

- [ ] Every Major Concern from input reports maps to at least one Q&A
- [ ] Every Referee Objection maps to at least one Q&A
- [ ] DA strongest counter-argument is addressed
- [ ] Key numbers extracted and tabulated
- [ ] Strategic guidance included for major questions
- [ ] Honest acknowledgment of limitations with concrete plans
- [ ] Follow-up chains for critical questions
- [ ] Paper-type appropriate questions used
